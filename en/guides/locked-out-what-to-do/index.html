First Steps

Before calling a locksmith, check these points:

Window left open?

Check if an accessible window was left open. The simplest and free solution!

Spare key?

A neighbor, relative or building manager might have a spare key.

Landlord / Agency?

If you're renting, your landlord or agency may have a spare.

Door just pushed?

Sometimes the door isn't really locked. Try gently pushing it.

What NOT to Do

Don't force the lock - You risk damaging it and increasing costs
Don't use a credit card - That only works in movies
Don't break the window - The costs will be much higher than a locksmith
Beware of "cheap" prices - Very low prices often hide scams

How to Choose a Good Locksmith

VAT number - A legal locksmith has a business registration (BCE)
Physical address - Beware of PO boxes or vague addresses
Price quoted upfront - Demand a quote BEFORE work starts
Reasonable surcharges - A night surcharge is normal, but should remain reasonable
Verifiable reviews - Check Google reviews, beware of fake ones

How Much Does Door Opening Cost?

Prices vary according to several factors:

Type of lock - A simple lock costs less than a high-security one
Time of intervention - Possible surcharge at night and weekends
Technique used - Non-destructive opening vs cylinder replacement

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a locked door be opened without breaking the lock?

Yes, in 95% of cases a professional locksmith opens the door without any damage using non-destructive techniques.

How long does it take to open a locked door?

The opening itself usually takes 5 to 15 minutes. The locksmith's arrival time is approximately 30 minutes.

Does my insurance cover the intervention?

Some home insurance policies include locksmith assistance. Check your contract or call your insurer.

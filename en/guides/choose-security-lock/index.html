Understanding Home Security

Choosing the right lock is one of the most important decisions you can make to protect your home. In Belgium, a burglary occurs every 4 minutes on average. Front doors remain the most common entry point for intruders, which is why investing in quality security hardware is essential.

However, the lock market can seem confusing: cylinder, multipoint, A2P certification, security levels... How do you navigate through all these technical terms? This comprehensive guide will help you understand the different options and choose the lock that best suits your situation, your door type, and your security needs.

Remember: even the best lock is only effective if installed correctly on a door that can withstand break-in attempts. A security chain is only as strong as its weakest link.

Types of Locks Explained

Before choosing, you need to understand the different lock types available:

European Cylinder

The most common lock type in Belgium and Europe. Easily recognizable by its distinctive shape with two different lengths on each side. The main advantage: replacement is simple and can be done without changing the entire lock mechanism. Available in different security levels.

Multipoint Lock

Locks the door at 3, 5, or even 7 different points along its height. Each locking point significantly increases resistance to forcing. Essential for exterior doors and highly recommended for apartment front doors. Requires professional installation.

A2P Certified Lock

The A2P label (Assurance Prévention Protection) is the French standard for lock security, widely recognized in Belgium. Locks are laboratory tested to verify their resistance to break-in attempts using various techniques. The gold standard for home security.

Smart Lock

Opens via smartphone app, code, fingerprint, or badge. Ideal for properties needing flexible access control: rental apartments, Airbnb, shared offices. Modern options combine convenience with security, though backup key access is recommended.

Mortise Lock

Embedded within the door rather than mounted on the surface. This traditional type offers excellent security when combined with quality cylinders. Commonly found in older Belgian buildings, often compatible with modern high-security cylinders.

Surface-Mounted Lock

Installed on the door surface rather than embedded. Often used as secondary locks to reinforce security. Visible from inside, which some consider a deterrent. Easy to install on doors where mortise locks aren't possible.

A2P Certification Explained

The A2P standard (Assurance Prévention Protection) is the reference for lock security in Belgium and France. Locks are tested by the CNPP (Centre National de Prévention et de Protection) under realistic break-in conditions:

A2P 1 Star

Resists 5 minutes against attempted break-in. Entry level security, suitable for internal doors or as secondary locks.

A2P 2 Stars

Resists 10 minutes. Recommended for apartment front doors and houses in low-risk areas. Good balance between security and budget.

A2P 3 Stars

Resists 15 minutes. Maximum protection for high-risk properties, isolated houses, or homes with valuables. The gold standard.

Important: an A2P lock provides optimal protection only if installed on a reinforced door. A high-security lock on a weak door is like a safe with a cardboard wall.

Understanding Cylinder Security

The cylinder is the heart of your lock – where you insert the key. Its quality directly determines your lock's resistance to manipulation:

Anti-picking pins - Special pins that resist the most common locksmith techniques. Essential against skilled burglars who prefer discrete entry without visible damage.
Anti-drill protection - Hardened steel elements that break drill bits. Drilling is a quick and common break-in method that this feature effectively counters.
Anti-snap technology - Prevents cylinder snapping, a common technique where burglars break the protruding part of the cylinder to access the mechanism.
Anti-bumping design - Resists the "bump key" technique, where a specially cut key and impact are used to align pins and open the lock.
Protected key profile - Keys that cannot be duplicated without authorization. Requires a security card to order copies, preventing unauthorized key duplication.
Reinforced housing - The external casing is made from hardened materials resistant to forced attacks with tools.

Which Lock for Which Situation

The ideal lock depends on your specific situation. Here are our recommendations:

City Apartment

3-point or 5-point multipoint lock with A2P 2 star cylinder minimum. Building entrance already provides first security barrier.

Detached House

A2P 3 star multipoint lock, reinforced strike plate, anti-drill cylinder. Consider reinforced door or armored door.

Isolated Property

Maximum security: A2P 3 star lock + armored door + alarm system. Multiple barriers are essential when response time is longer.

Cellar / Garage

Minimum 3-point lock, anti-drill cylinder. Often neglected but frequently targeted entry points.

Commercial Premises

A2P 2 star lock minimum + metal shutter. Consider insurance requirements which often specify security standards.

Rental Property

Smart lock for easy access management. Change codes between tenants without changing locks.

Don't Forget the Door

The most secure lock is useless if your door isn't solid. Here's what to verify:

Door Material

Solid wood, steel, or reinforced composite are best. Hollow wood or thin panels can be kicked in regardless of lock quality. Consider replacing if your door is weak.

Door Frame

The frame must be as solid as the door. A weak frame can be forced even with the best lock. Reinforce with metal plates if necessary.

Strike Plate

Where the lock bolts engage. A reinforced strike plate with long screws into the frame studs significantly increases kick-resistance.

Hinges

Security hinges with non-removable pins prevent the door being lifted off. Essential for outward-opening doors where hinges are exposed.

Glass Panels

Any glass near the lock is a vulnerability. Consider security film, bars, or replacing with reinforced glass if necessary.

Mail Slot

Can be used to reach inside and manipulate handles or locks. Install a mail cage or consider moving the mailbox outside.

Professional Installation Matters

Why professional installation is crucial for security locks:

Correct alignment - A misaligned lock is easier to force and wears faster. Professional installation ensures perfect fit.
Proper reinforcement - Professionals reinforce weak points in the door and frame during installation.
Right product selection - A locksmith can assess your door and recommend compatible, appropriate security solutions.
Warranty validity - Many manufacturers require professional installation for warranty coverage.
Insurance compliance - Some policies require professionally installed locks meeting specific standards for burglary coverage.

Additional Security Measures

Complement your locks with these additional security measures:

Door chain or limiter - Allows partially opening the door to verify visitors before fully unlocking. Simple but effective.
Peephole or video doorbell - See who's at the door without opening. Modern video doorbells record footage and send alerts.
Alarm system - Serves as second line of defense. Many burglars flee immediately when an alarm triggers.
Motion-sensor lighting - Illuminates entrances at night. Burglars prefer working in darkness.
Security cameras - Visible cameras deter, hidden cameras provide evidence. Check GDPR rules for outdoor placement.
Neighborhood watch - Alert neighbors are excellent protection. Exchange numbers and report suspicious activity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best lock for a front door?

For a front door, we recommend a multipoint lock (3 or 5 points) with an A2P 2 star cylinder minimum. For isolated houses or ground floor apartments, consider A2P 3 stars. The lock should be matched to a solid door and reinforced frame for maximum protection.

Is an A2P lock worth the investment?

A2P certification guarantees tested resistance against real break-in attempts. While certified locks cost more, they provide documented protection that basic locks cannot match. For front doors and security-critical applications, A2P certification is strongly recommended.

Can I install a security lock myself?

Simple cylinder replacements can be DIY if you're handy. However, multipoint locks and high-security installations require professional fitting to ensure proper alignment, reinforcement, and function. Poor installation can void warranties and actually reduce security.

How often should locks be replaced?

Quality locks can last 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Replace sooner if: keys become difficult to turn, the lock shows signs of forced entry attempt, you've lost keys, or after a burglary. Also consider upgrading if your locks are older basic models without modern security features.

What's the difference between a lock and a cylinder?

The lock is the complete mechanism embedded in or mounted on the door. The cylinder is the part where you insert the key – the "brain" of the lock. Often you can significantly upgrade security by replacing just the cylinder with a high-security version, without changing the entire lock.

Are smart locks as secure as traditional locks?

Quality smart locks can match traditional locks in physical security, with added benefits like access logs and remote control. However, they introduce electronic vulnerabilities. Choose reputable brands, use strong passwords, keep firmware updated, and always have a backup key system.

My insurance requires specific lock standards – what do I need?

Check your policy carefully. Many Belgian insurers require A2P certified locks for burglary coverage. Some specify star levels based on property type or location. We can assess your current locks and recommend compliant upgrades. Keep purchase receipts and installation certificates.

What should I do after moving into a new home?

Always change or re-key locks when moving in. You don't know who has copies of existing keys – previous owners, their family, contractors, estate agents. This is a basic security measure that's often overlooked. Consider it part of moving costs.

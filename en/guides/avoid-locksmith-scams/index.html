Understanding the Problem

The emergency locksmith sector unfortunately suffers from an alarming number of fraudulent actors. Each year in Belgium, thousands of consumers fall victim to dishonest locksmiths who exploit their vulnerability during a stressful moment. When you're locked out of your home, especially late at night or during the weekend, clear thinking becomes difficult.

Scammers have understood this perfectly: they prey on your urgency and stress to charge excessive rates and often perform unnecessary work. The victims often only realize the deception once the invoice is paid, sometimes under threat. It's difficult to think clearly when you're outside in the cold with children waiting or an early morning appointment the next day.

This guide will help you recognize the warning signs of a locksmith scam, protect yourself before calling, and know what to do if you find yourself in a problematic situation. Prevention is your best weapon against these unscrupulous individuals.

Warning Signs of a Scam

Scammers use identifiable techniques. Know how to spot these red flags before it's too late:

Abnormally low phone quote

The classic bait: a ridiculously low quote to secure your call. Upon arrival, the bill magically multiplies. "Oh, it's more complicated than expected", "this lock requires special equipment"... Excuses are inexhaustible.

Refusal to provide a written quote

A scammer refuses to commit in writing. "I have to see it first" is their favorite phrase. Yet a professional can estimate a standard opening over the phone based on your lock type.

Immediate destruction of the lock

Without even trying non-destructive techniques, the scammer drills or breaks your lock. Result: you now "must" buy an overpriced lock from him. This is a classic fraud scheme.

"The lock must be changed"

Once your door is open, they claim the lock is "damaged", "worn", or "dangerous to keep". In reality, 95% of locks can be opened without damage by a competent professional.

Refusal to provide a VAT invoice

"I'll give you a discount if you pay cash" - this classic phrase hides illegal practice. Without an invoice, you have no legal recourse if there's a problem.

Threats and intimidation

The scammer becomes aggressive when you question the bill. Threats to call the police, refusal to leave, intimidation... A true professional never behaves this way with their clients.

Untraceable phone number

Mobile phone numbers, prepaid phones, unverifiable numbers. Serious locksmiths have landlines and traceable contact information.

Google Ads flooding

Scam companies invest heavily in online advertising to be at the top of results. The first result is not always the most trustworthy.

How Scam Networks Operate

It's important to understand how these fraudulent networks work to better protect yourself:

Call centers disguised as craftsmen - Many "locksmiths" are actually call centers that subcontract work to untrained individuals. They pocket a large percentage of each intervention.
Aggressive SEO and artificial reviews - They create multiple websites with different names, pay for Google rankings, and publish fake 5-star reviews. Everything is designed to appear legitimate.
Multiplying names - The same scammer may operate under a dozen different company names. If one gets a bad reputation, they simply create another.
Shell companies - Companies are sometimes registered in the name of individuals living abroad, making legal action impossible.
Pressure sales techniques - Once on-site, the scammer uses proven sales techniques: urgency, fear, authority. "You can't spend the night with this lock", "your insurance won't cover you"...

How to Protect Yourself Before Calling

Take a few minutes, even in an emergency, to perform these checks:

Verify the VAT number - Every Belgian business has a VAT number starting with BE. Check it on the FPS Economy BCE database. It takes 30 seconds and can save you from disaster.
Look for a physical address - A real locksmith has a physical workshop, not just a phone number. Check that the address exists and corresponds to a professional premises.
Read reviews carefully - Beware of profiles with only 5-star reviews all recent. Real reviews are varied, detailed, and spread over time. Check Trustpilot, Google Maps, Facebook.
Demand the total price by phone - Insist on getting the complete price including travel, labor, and any possible supplements. An honest professional can estimate a standard opening.
Ask about payment methods - A serious professional accepts card and issues invoices. "Cash only" is a major red flag.
Check if they have a marked vehicle - True professionals invest in their image: marked vehicle, uniform, professional tools. Scammers arrive in unmarked cars.

When the Locksmith Arrives

Even after your checks, stay vigilant during the intervention:

Request ID

Ask to see the professional's ID card. Note their name, first name, and the company's VAT number. A professional will be happy to provide this information.

Photograph the lock before

Before they touch anything, take clear photos of your lock. This is irrefutable proof in case of intentional damage.

Confirm the price

Before any intervention, ask for written confirmation of the quoted price. If they refuse or the price has changed, stop everything.

Watch the work

Stay present during the entire intervention. A scammer will not dare to intentionally damage a lock under your surveillance.

Question any extra charges

Every extra charge must be justified. "Special equipment", "unusual lock", "night supplements" must have been announced in advance.

Refuse any non-agreed work

If they want to change the lock "for your safety", demand a written quote and time to think. Never accept under pressure.

What to Do If You're a Victim

If despite your precautions you've been scammed, here's what to do:

Don't pay under pressure - If the amount is abusive and the locksmith threatens you, call the police immediately (101). You have the right to dispute an unfair bill.
Gather all evidence - Keep the invoice (even if abusive), take photos of the work done, note the locksmith's contact details, time, and any witnesses.
File a police complaint - Go to your local police station to report the scam. This report is essential for any subsequent proceedings.

Warn others - Leave an honest review on Google, report the company to consumer associations. Your testimony can save other victims.

Your Rights as a Consumer

Belgian law protects you. Here are your rights:

Right to a detailed quote - Any professional must provide you with a detailed quote before performing work. Refusal is illegal.
Right to a proper invoice - The invoice must include: company name, VAT number, full address, detail of services, unit price, VAT, total.
Right to refuse additional work - No one can force you to accept work that wasn't agreed upon. You can refuse and only pay for the initial intervention.
Right to pay after the job - Paying after completion is normal. No deposit before intervention except for special orders.
Protection against unfair practices - Excessive prices, misleading advertising, and threats are illegal. You can take legal action.

How to Find a Reliable Locksmith

Criteria for choosing a trustworthy professional:

Local establishment - A locksmith established in your area has a reputation to maintain. Check how long the company has existed.
Professional certifications - Membership in trade federations, specific training, recognized certifications. These are signs of seriousness.
Transparent pricing - A reliable locksmith isn't afraid to discuss prices. They'll give you a clear estimate over the phone.
Diverse and authentic reviews - Check reviews on multiple platforms. Look for detailed reviews mentioning specific interventions.
Word of mouth - Ask your neighbors, family, friends. A locksmith recommended by someone you trust is gold.
Save their number - Once you've found a reliable professional, save their number. You won't have to search in an emergency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify if a locksmith is reliable?

Check their VAT number on the BCE (Crossroads Bank for Enterprises), read reviews on Google Maps and Trustpilot, verify they have a physical address, and demand a complete quote over the phone before any intervention. A serious professional has nothing to hide.

What if the locksmith refuses to give a price?

Hang up and call another locksmith. A serious professional can always estimate the cost of a standard intervention. Refusal to quote is the first sign of a scam. Don't let the urgency force you into a bad decision.

Can a locksmith refuse to give me an invoice?

No, it's completely illegal. Every professional registered for VAT must provide a detailed invoice with their VAT number. Refusal to invoice should alert you to the legality of the business and disqualify them immediately.

What are the warning signs of a locksmith scam?

The main signs are: abnormally low quoted price, refusal to give a written quote, systematic destruction of the lock, claims that replacement is mandatory, only cash payment, threats if you question the bill, and mobile phone numbers without verifiable establishment.

How do I file a complaint against a fraudulent locksmith?

Gather all evidence (invoice, photos, witnesses), go to your police station to file a complaint, then report the business on pointdecontact.belgique.be. You can also contact a consumer association for assistance in your proceedings.

Can I dispute a payment made to a fraudulent locksmith?

If you paid by card, contact your bank to check the possibility of a chargeback. You'll need to provide evidence of the fraud. For cash payment, recovery is more complex and requires legal action. This is why it's important to always pay by card when possible.

Why do scammers always want to change the lock?

Because it's the most profitable operation for them. They sell you a basic lock at an excessive price and charge for installation. Yet in 95% of cases, a competent professional can open a door without any damage to the lock.

Does my insurance cover intervention by a fraudulent locksmith?

Generally no. Insurers reimburse fees from legitimate professionals with proper invoicing. A fraudulent locksmith's invoice may be refused. This is an additional reason to always choose a registered and invoicing professional.
